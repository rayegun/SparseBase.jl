SparseBase.mapstored! — Function
mapstored[!](f, [E], [C], A)
mapstored(f, [E], A ∪ B)
mapstored(f, [E], (A ∩ B) ∪ C)

Map a function over the stored values of A(, B, C, ...).

Unions and intersections of A, B, C... operate elementwise over the unions and intersections of the patterns of those matrices.

Issues:

  1. This could potentially lead to wrapper hell. I hope it doesn't, I think we would have

UnionPattern, IntersectionPattern (maybe ComplementPattern?). I don't think these wrappers are quite as bad as the ones in LinAlg, but I could be wrong.

Current implementations (SSGrB) only implement mapstored[!](f, [C], A), mapstored[!](f, [C], A ∪ B) and mapstored[!](f, [C], A ∩ B)
